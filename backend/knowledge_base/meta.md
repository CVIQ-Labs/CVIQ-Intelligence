# Meta — Hiring Culture & CV Tips

## What Meta Looks For

Meta hires on two primary dimensions across all roles:
1. **Impact** — what did you ship, and did it move the needle at scale?
2. **People skills** — can you work across functions and drive alignment?

Meta operates at a scale most companies never reach (3+ billion users). Candidates who can't demonstrate experience at scale — or at least evidence of thinking at scale — tend not to get through.

## Meta's Engineering Culture

### Scale Is Everything
Meta's core systems serve billions of users. Engineering candidates are expected to have:
- Experience with high-throughput, low-latency systems
- Evidence of thinking about failure modes, fallbacks, and degraded states
- Work at companies or on projects with meaningful user or data scale

### Bootcamp Culture
New SWEs join Meta's Bootcamp — a 6-week internal rotation where engineers pick their team. CV context matters: generalist engineers who've worked across backend, infra, and data do better in Bootcamp matching than narrow specialists.

### Open Source and Community
Meta values engineers who contribute back to the community. Open source contributions, internal tooling that became external (React, PyTorch, Cassandra were Meta-born), and conference speaking signal genuine passion.

## Meta Engineering Levels
- E3: New grad / junior (often from Bootcamp)
- E4: Mid-level, owns features
- E5: Senior, owns systems or domains
- E6: Staff, cross-team technical leadership
- E7: Principal, org-wide technical direction
- E8+: Distinguished Engineer / Fellow

## CV Tips for Meta SWE Roles
- Show scale explicitly: "served 40M+ requests/day", "supported 200K+ concurrent users"
- Demonstrate ownership of full features or systems, not just components
- Open source contributions to major projects (especially React, PyTorch) are notable
- Systems design experience is tested heavily — show it on the CV
- Internal tooling and platform engineering (infra, developer experience) are valued at Meta

## CV Tips for Meta PM Roles
- Meta APM programme is highly competitive (grad entry)
- Show data-driven decision making throughout — no metric-free bullets
- Social product thinking: network effects, virality, engagement loops
- Comfortable with 0-to-1 and scale-up phases — note which phase your products were in
- Family of Apps (Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, Messenger) and Reality Labs (VR/AR) have different cultures

## Keywords That Resonate at Meta
- Scale, billions, distributed systems, reliability, latency, throughput, infra
- React, PyTorch, Hack, Python, C++, GraphQL
- A/B testing, experimentation, feature flag, engagement, DAU, MAU, retention
- Cross-functional, alignment, roadmap, growth, monetisation
- AR/VR, Metaverse, Reality Labs (if targeting hardware/XR roles)

## ATS Notes
Meta uses Workday. Sourcing is also heavy via LinkedIn Recruiter and GitHub. CVs reviewed by technical sourcers before hiring managers.

## Common Rejection Reasons at Meta
- No evidence of scale (even at a small company, describe user counts, data volume, traffic)
- Impact not stated — Meta interviewers specifically probe "what was the impact of your work"
- Generic software engineering bullets with no differentiation
- Mismatch between claimed seniority and evidence on CV (claiming E6 with E4-level bullets)
- Stability concerns: multiple sub-12-month tenures (Meta values commitment, especially post-2022 layoffs)
