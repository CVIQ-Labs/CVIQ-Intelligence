# Finance Analyst — CV Expectations & Hiring Criteria

## What Recruiters Look For (First 6 Seconds)
- Degree from a target institution (for front-office banking) or strong GPA
- Evidence of financial modelling: DCF, LBO, three-statement models
- Excel and quantitative skills — SQL increasingly expected
- Internship or junior experience at a recognised firm
- Sector or product coverage (IB, AM, corp fin, FP&A, credit)

## Must-Have CV Sections
- **Education** — degree, institution, classification/GPA, relevant modules for juniors
- **Experience** — deal exposure with context, company names, transaction sizes
- **Technical Skills** — Excel (advanced), financial modelling, Bloomberg, SQL, Python/VBA optional
- **Certifications** — CFA progress, ACCA/ACA progress, Bloomberg certification
- **Activities** — investment society, finance case competitions, relevant volunteering

## Role Types and What Each Looks For

### Investment Banking Analyst
- Pitch book and deal execution experience (M&A, ECM, DCM)
- Overnight modelling, due diligence, client materials
- Deal sizes mentioned: "advised on £450M Series C" or "supported M&A transaction valued at $2.1B"
- Banks use headcount and deal count to assess firm prestige

### FP&A / Corporate Finance Analyst
- P&L ownership, budget variance analysis, forecast modelling
- Business partnering experience (communicating to non-finance stakeholders)
- ERP system experience: SAP, Oracle, Workday Adaptive
- "Reduced forecast error by X%" type bullets are strong

### Credit / Risk Analyst
- Credit assessment, underwriting, loan book management
- Risk frameworks (Basel III/IV, IFRS 9)
- Sector knowledge (corporate, leveraged finance, real estate, structured products)
- Python or R for risk modelling is a differentiator

### Asset Management / Buy Side
- Investment thesis development, portfolio analysis
- Bloomberg Terminal proficiency is expected
- CFA progress is a significant signal
- "Generated alpha of X% vs benchmark" if applicable

## Common Weaknesses in Finance Analyst CVs
- No deal sizes or financial figures ("worked on transactions" — meaningless without scale)
- Missing GPA when it's above 3.5 (GPA omission for strong candidates is always a mistake)
- Generic bullets that could describe any analyst anywhere
- No mention of financial model type (everyone does "modelling" — specify DCF, LBO, comps)
- Listing Excel as a skill without demonstrating what was built in it

## Strong Bullet Examples
- "Built three-statement LBO model for $340M acquisition, stress-tested against 5 leverage scenarios"
- "Produced weekly FP&A variance report for 6 business units, reducing CFO review time by 40%"
- "Underwrote 12 corporate credit facilities with aggregate value of £180M; 0 defaults in portfolio"
- "Screened 200+ companies weekly using Bloomberg screens to identify undervalued mid-cap opportunities"

## Keywords Recruiters and ATS Look For
Modelling: DCF, LBO, M&A, comps, three-statement model, valuation, financial modelling, sensitivity analysis
Tools: Excel, Bloomberg, Capital IQ, FactSet, VBA, Python, SQL, SAP, Workday
Finance: P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, EBITDA, IRR, NPV, NAV, AUM, credit analysis
Processes: due diligence, pitch book, deal execution, roadshow, underwriting, portfolio management

## Red Flags
- GPA below 2:1 (UK) or 3.3 GPA (US) for front-office roles — expect screening out
- Job tenure under 12 months at multiple firms
- No exposure to financial statements or modelling of any kind
- Skills listed with no evidence of how they were applied
