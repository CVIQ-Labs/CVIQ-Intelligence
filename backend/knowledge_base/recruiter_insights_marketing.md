# Recruiter Insights — Marketing Industry

## How Marketing Recruiters Screen CVs

### What Marketing Recruiters Look For First
1. **Channels owned** — SEO, PPC, email, social, content, ABM, performance — must be specific
2. **Metrics driven** — CTR, CAC, ROAS, MQL, pipeline contribution, conversion rates
3. **Tools and platforms** — HubSpot, Salesforce, GA4, Marketo, Semrush, Meta Ads Manager
4. **Brand or company type** — B2B vs B2C experience matters; DTC vs enterprise matters
5. **Budget managed** — "managed £500K PPC budget" signals seniority and accountability

### The Marketing CV Scan
Marketing recruiters spend time looking for one thing above all else: **proof of results**. The biggest mistake on a marketing CV is describing activities rather than outcomes:
- Weak: "Managed social media accounts for the company"
- Strong: "Grew Instagram following from 8K to 45K in 6 months, increasing referred revenue by 22%"

### What Gets a Marketing CV Rejected Fast
- No metrics anywhere on the CV
- Generic channel experience with no depth ("experience in social media")
- Missing tools — every marketing role expects a specific stack; showing none signals inexperience
- Portfolio/links broken or not included (for content, design, or creative roles)
- Over-emphasis on responsibilities rather than business outcomes

## Marketing Specialist Areas and What Recruiters Want

### Performance / Growth Marketing
- ROAS, CPA, CAC, LTV are the core metrics
- Platform certifications add credibility: Google Ads, Meta Blueprint, HubSpot
- A/B testing experience is expected at mid-level and above
- Attribution modelling and incrementality testing signal seniority

### Content and SEO
- Domain authority improvements, keyword rankings, organic traffic growth
- Writing samples or portfolio link required; a CV without a link for content roles is a flag
- Technical SEO (Core Web Vitals, crawlability, schema) distinguishes senior candidates

### Brand and Communications
- Campaign names and reach metrics
- Agency experience vs. in-house experience — both valued but assessed differently
- Press hits, awards, or brand recognition moments signal strong execution

### Product Marketing
- Launch experience — what was launched, what was the outcome
- Sales enablement, competitive positioning, and messaging frameworks
- GTM strategy ownership shows seniority

## ATS Reality in Marketing
Marketing roles tend to use Workday or Greenhouse. Keywords that matter:
Digital marketing, SEO, SEM, PPC, email marketing, content strategy, HubSpot, Salesforce, Google Analytics, conversion rate optimisation, demand generation, brand management, campaign management, A/B testing, social media marketing, paid social, paid search.

## Red Flags in Marketing CVs
- No link to portfolio for creative or content roles
- Only listing tools without specifying outcomes achieved with them
- CVs that are longer than 2 pages for under 8 years experience
- Claiming ownership of brand growth with no numbers to back it
- Listing channels without specifying B2B vs B2C context (very different skills)

## Seniority Signals in Marketing
- **Executive/Coordinator**: channel support, scheduling, reporting, content creation
- **Manager**: channel ownership, campaign delivery, basic budget management
- **Senior Manager/Head**: multi-channel strategy, team management, budget ownership
- **Director/VP**: full-funnel strategy, cross-functional leadership, revenue contribution
