# McKinsey & Company — Hiring Culture & CV Tips

## What McKinsey Looks For

McKinsey hires on three criteria across all roles:
1. **Problem-solving ability** — structured, data-driven, first-principles thinking
2. **Leadership and personal impact** — driving change, influencing without authority
3. **Entrepreneurial drive** — initiative, high standards, restlessness with the status quo

McKinsey's interview process is heavily case-based. The CV needs to demonstrate that you can think analytically and lead — everything else gets tested live in the interview.

## McKinsey's Hiring Tiers

### Consulting (BA / Junior Associate / Associate / Engagement Manager)
- **BA (Business Analyst)**: undergraduate or masters entry. GPA 3.7+ from target school typically required.
- **Junior Associate**: rare path for MBAs with < 3 years experience
- **Associate**: post-MBA or PhD with 2-3 years experience
- **Engagement Manager**: post-MBA with 3-5 years of relevant experience or internal promotion

### Specialist Roles (Analytics, Digital, Implementation, Design)
- Less emphasis on target school; more emphasis on domain expertise
- Analytics: Python, SQL, data modelling, statistical analysis
- Digital: software engineering, cloud, agile, product management
- Implementation: change management, programme delivery, stakeholder management

## CV Tips for McKinsey Applications

### Structure and Format
- One page for undergraduates and recent MBAs
- Two pages maximum for senior hires
- Reverse chronological; education first for candidates under 5 years experience
- Use bullet points, never paragraphs
- McKinsey uses a very strict internal CV template — match it as closely as possible

### The McKinsey CV Formula
Every bullet should follow: **Action + Approach + Impact**
- "Led restructuring of client's supply chain operations, analysing 12 supplier contracts and renegotiating terms to achieve $4.2M annual cost reduction"
- "Developed go-to-market strategy for FMCG client entering 3 new markets, resulting in €18M incremental revenue in year one"

### What McKinsey Readers Look For
McKinsey interviewers specifically look for:
- **Leadership stories**: when did you step up? Take initiative? Drive something that wasn't your job?
- **Impact with numbers**: every project has an outcome — what was it in £/$/%?
- **Intellectual ability signals**: top grades, competitive programmes, academic prizes, research
- **Breadth of impact**: did you influence only yourself, your team, your company, or a whole sector?

## McKinsey-Specific Keywords and Signals
Problem-solving: structured analysis, hypothesis-driven, root cause analysis, frameworks
Leadership: cross-functional teams, stakeholder management, change management, workshops
Impact: revenue growth, cost reduction, EBITDA improvement, operational efficiency
Programmes: McKinsey Forward, McKinsey Solve, Diversity Leadership programmes

## Red Flags for McKinsey
- GPA below 3.7 / 2:1 First Class equivalent (for BA or Associate)
- No leadership stories — every strong candidate has been a captain, president, founder, or lead
- Bullets that describe tasks, not outcomes ("analysed data" instead of "identified $3M cost saving")
- No evidence of intellectual rigour — missing academic prizes, publications, competitive results
- Career narrative that doesn't make sense for consulting (e.g., 10 years in one niche with no lateral thinking)

## McKinsey Digital and Analytics
McKinsey has a large and growing tech/analytics practice:
- QuantumBlack: data science, ML, AI for client engagements
- McKinsey Digital: technology strategy, agile transformation, cloud
- Implementation: large-scale programme delivery

For these tracks, Python, SQL, machine learning, and cloud experience are expected alongside consulting fundamentals. The case interview still applies but is adapted to the technical role.

## Consulting vs. Industry Comparisons
McKinsey interviewers will ask: "Why consulting instead of staying in industry?" Your CV should make this easy to answer:
- Show intellectual curiosity across multiple domains
- Show exposure to senior stakeholders early in your career
- Show that you thrive in ambiguous, project-based environments
