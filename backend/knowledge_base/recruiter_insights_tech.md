# Recruiter Insights — Tech Industry

## How Tech Recruiters Actually Screen CVs

### The 6-Second Scan
Recruiters spend an average of 6 seconds on initial screening. In that time they look at:
1. Job title (does it match what they're hiring?)
2. Company names (brand recognition signals credibility)
3. Tenure (job hopper red flag or solid track record?)
4. Education (degree level and institution)
5. One or two bullet points (are there numbers or just vague descriptions?)

If all five pass, the CV moves to the next stage. If any one fails, it's rejected in seconds.

### What Gets a CV Into the "Yes" Pile Fast
- Job title matches the role almost exactly (or is one level below)
- At least one FAANG, unicorn, or well-known product company in history
- Quantified bullets with real numbers (%, $, users, latency, time saved)
- Clean formatting — no tables, no graphics, easy to skim
- Skills section with relevant tech stack in the first 5 words

### What Gets a CV Rejected in Under 10 Seconds
- No quantified bullets anywhere on the CV
- Current title wildly different from the role applied for
- CV longer than 2 pages for under 8 years experience
- Dates missing — always raises suspicion
- Summary paragraph that says nothing specific ("passionate problem solver")

## How the Tech Recruiting Process Works

### Typical Funnel
1. **ATS screen** — automated keyword match; CV may never be seen by human if keywords are missing
2. **Recruiter screen** — 15-30 min call to check baseline: salary, location, notice period, basics
3. **Hiring manager review** — actual technical bar set here; they look for specifics
4. **Technical interviews** — typically 3-5 rounds for SWE roles
5. **Offer** — total comp discussion

### What Recruiters Are Briefed On
Recruiters are given a role brief with: must-have skills, nice-to-haves, seniority level, red flags to watch, and sometimes "type" (startup scrappy vs. enterprise methodical). They filter CVs against this brief, not against their own intuition.

### How Recruiters Use LinkedIn
Most tech recruiters cross-reference the CV with LinkedIn:
- Dates must match
- Job titles must be consistent
- Unexplained gaps in one but not the other raise flags
- GitHub links get clicked if they're on the CV

## Red Flags Tech Recruiters Flag to Hiring Managers
- Progression that doesn't make sense (senior at 1 company, junior at the next)
- Only consulting/agency experience for a product company role
- CV that looks like it was reused from 5 years ago (technologies listed are outdated)
- Company names that don't exist on LinkedIn or seem inflated
- Bullet points that describe the team's output, not the candidate's contribution

## ATS Reality in Tech
Most tech companies use Greenhouse, Lever, or Workday. These systems:
- Parse CV text and match against keywords in the job description
- Rank candidates by keyword density in some configurations
- Strip formatting, tables, and columns — plain text survives best
- Do NOT understand synonyms (e.g. "ML" and "machine learning" are different strings)

Write your skills section with both the acronym and the full term where relevant.

## What Senior Tech Recruiters Care About at Different Levels
- **IC roles (L3-L5)**: output quality, stack alignment, evidence of learning
- **Senior/Staff roles (L5-L7)**: architectural decisions, cross-team impact, mentorship
- **Engineering management**: team size, delivery track record, how they handle conflict
- **VP+**: org building, strategy, business alignment
