# Recruiter Insights — Finance Industry

## How Finance Recruiters Screen CVs

### The Finance CV Standard
Finance is one of the most standardised industries for CVs. Recruiters expect:
- Reverse chronological order, no exceptions
- Education at the top for candidates under 5 years experience (with GPA if above 3.5)
- Precise dates with no gaps
- Quantified bullet points: deal sizes, AUM, P&L impact, cost savings
- Clean, single-column layout — tables and graphics are penalised in ATS

### What Finance Recruiters Look For First
1. **Institution name** — target schools (LSE, Oxbridge, Imperial, Ivy League) matter more in finance than tech
2. **GPA / degree classification** — First Class or 2:1 expected; below 2:2 is usually screened out at bulge bracket banks
3. **Internship pedigree** — Goldman, JPMorgan, Citi, BlackRock, McKinsey spring weeks signal early filtering
4. **Certifications** — CFA Level 1+ is noticed; ACCA/ACA for accounting; CPA for US roles
5. **Deal or transaction experience** — specific deal sizes, sectors, and outcomes

### What Gets Rejected Fast in Finance
- GPA not listed when it should be (raises suspicion it's low)
- Bullet points without deal sizes, $ amounts, or quantified outcomes
- Overly creative formatting — conservative layouts are expected and preferred
- Job titles from non-standard firms with no explanation of the firm's size or scope
- Summary statements that are generic ("detail-oriented, results-driven professional")

## Finance Recruiting Process

### Investment Banking
- Applications open very early (summer internships at bulge brackets recruit up to 18 months ahead)
- Online application → numerical reasoning/verbal reasoning tests → HireVue video → Superday interviews
- Network matters: informational interviews and referrals significantly improve odds

### Asset Management / Buy Side
- Less structured than IB but equally pedigree-driven
- Emphasis on investment thesis, stock pitch ability, portfolio thinking
- CFA progress is taken seriously — list it clearly with level and date

### FinTech
- Hybrid of finance and tech recruiting; ATS similar to tech companies
- SQL and Python increasingly expected even for finance analyst roles
- Agile, product thinking, and startup experience valued more than in traditional finance

## ATS Reality in Finance
Large financial institutions use Oracle Taleo or SAP SuccessFactors — older and less intelligent than Greenhouse. These systems:
- Are highly keyword-dependent
- Often require re-entering CV data into forms manually
- Rank candidates by keyword matches to job description

Essential keywords to include: financial modelling, valuation, DCF, LBO, pitch book, Bloomberg, Excel, VBA, SQL, portfolio analysis, risk management.

## What Finance Recruiters Flag as Red Flags
- Gaps in employment history with no explanation (finance is conservative on this)
- Job tenure under 12 months at more than one firm (commitment concern)
- Mixing up financial terms incorrectly — signals lack of genuine finance experience
- No mention of deal exposure, asset class, or sector for front-office roles
- Missing or vague degree classification

## Key Credentials and When to List Them
- **CFA**: list as "CFA Level X Candidate" with expected pass date, or "CFA Charterholder"
- **ACCA/ACA**: list progress clearly — number of exams passed
- **MBA**: top business schools (LBS, INSEAD, Wharton, HBS) are significant signals
- **Bloomberg**: list certification if you have it — often required in AM roles
